Fairuz

the Arab world's most famous singer...

Fairuz performing at one of her Beirut comeback concerts.

Fairouz's crystalline voice – her stage name means "turquoise" in Arabic – and her haunting lyrics about love, life, Lebanon and Jerusalem, have made her an icon second only to the legendary Egyptian singer Umm Kulthum, mourned by millions when she died. Like her, Fairouz, who is Lebanese by origin and is now 83, is loved and feted as a national treasure across the Arabic-speaking world – and known and admired far beyond it.

Here's a time line of Fairuz

    1935-1950s

  • 1934 - November 21, Nouhad Haddad is born in 'Jabal Al Arz' village, Lebanon.
  • 1946 - Mohammad Fleifel (oriental music teacher) notices young Nouhad's talents. She becomes his student at the Conservatoire Libanais.
  • 1949 - First song, composed by Assi Rahbani and written by Moukarzel, "Ghouroub" (Sunset).
  • 1952 - November, her first huge success, "Itab" (Reproaches), a song composed by the Rahbani brothers. Fairuz becomes a major artistic figure in the Middle-East.
  • 1955 - January 23, Fairuz marries Assi Rahbani.
  • 1957
    • Fairuz faces an audience for the first time during the show "Ayyam Al Hassad" (Days of Harvest) where she sings "Lebnan Ya Akhdar Helou" (Lebanon the Beautiful Green) in the Baalbeck International Festival.
    • Awarded "Cavalier" Order Medal of Honor by Lebanese President Camille Chamoun.
    • The Rahbanis turn down a request for Fairuz to sing at the presidential palace at the honor of the Shah of Iran, who is visiting at that time.
  • 1959
    • Baalbeck International Festival, where she performs "Al 'Urs Fil Qarya" (The Wedding in the Village) [also called "Al Muhakama" (The Trial)].
    • First appearance in Damascus International Festival.

    • 1960s

  • 1961 - Performances in Canada, United States, Brazil and Argentina.
  • 1962
    • Fairuz performs at Royal Albert Hall in London.
    • Fairuz's sales pass the 10 million mark to become the first Arab female singer to achieve this number after Umm Kulthum.
    • She is awarded the Order of Merit by Lebanese President Fouad Chehab.
  • 1963
    • Order of Cedars, awarded by Lebanese President Fouad Chehab.
    • Receives the Medal of Honor awarded by King Hussein of Jordan.
  • 1966 - In Damascus where she sings "The Bells of the Return" for the Palestinian cause.
  • 1967
    • She receives the Order of Merit, First Class, awarded by President Nureddine al'Attassi, Syria.
    • "Safarbarlek" (The Exile) is made into a movie.
  • 1968 - She receives the Key of the Holy City, awarded by the Cultural Committee of Jerusalem.

    • 1970s

  • 1970 - She receives the Legion of Honour awarded by Lebanese President Suleiman Frangieh.
  • 1971
    • A sold out 11 city tour in the United States, Canada and Mexico, during which she sings selections from "Nass min Wara".
    • Release of 'Fairuz in America' a documentary about her tour, produced by Parker and Associates. The documentary becomes TV’s highest rated show in the Arab World.
  • 1975
    • She receives the Gold Medal of Honour awarded by King Hussein, Jordan
    • Fairuz refuses to sing at the honour of the Shah of Iran in the prestigious Phoenicia Hotel in Beirut during his visit to Lebanon.
  • 1977 - Good Friday prayers held in Sidnaya Holy shrine in Syria. The Holy Seraphim Monastery is opened for the first time in 150 years for Fairouz to pray in. The area is shut down one week before the event for security reasons as about 1,600 people camp around the premises. About 6,500 people attend the event and for the first time she sings "La Tanouhi" (Don't Weep).
  • 1977 - 1978 - Her last musical play, "Petra"
  • 1978 - In the London Palladium, where she first sings "The Bus"; her last appearance with Nasri Shamseddine.

    • 1980s

  • 1985 - August: In Bousra Roman Amphitheatre, Syria, the biggest venue ever held in the Middle East; entrance is free and there are no tickets. About 30,000 people camp outside several days before the event. On the day, 13,000 fill up the stadium by dawn and all roads to the area are completely shut down. The crowds are out of control and beyond reason with elation as they carry Fairuz’s car on their shoulders after the concert.
  • 1988 - She receives the Commandeur des Arts et des Lettres awarded by president François Mitterrand, France.

    • 1990s

  • 1993 - Fairuz sues Madonna for $2.5 million for plagiarism over the Arab section/sampling in her song "Erotica". The song contains a section of a Christian Great Friday hymn that translates "Today, He is held to a cross" while Madonna repeatedly chants over Fairuz's voice 'All over me'
  • 1994 - Fairuz is the star of the launch event of the Solidere Real Estates Company. Her first concert in Lebanon since the end of the war is held in Martyrs' Square, the separation area between East and West Beirut, to reunite the divided nation.
  • 1997
    • She receives the Highest Artistic Distinction award by president of Tunisia Ben Ali.
    • Jerusalem Award is awarded by the Culture and Arts Committee, Jerusalem.
  • 1998
    • In Tunisia for the first time since 1968.
    • The release of "Fairuz"—a documentary about her childhood, life with her husband and brothers-in-law and how she became the symbol of a dying nation’s hope.
    • She receives the Chevalier de la Légion d'honneur awarded by French president Jacques Chirac.
    • Highest Distinction awarded by King Hussein, Jordan
  • 1999
    • At the MGM Grand Arena, Las Vegas, where she is reported to have brought in the biggest crowd since Frank Sinatra. Well over 14,000 people were estimated to be inside the stadium and over 5,000 outside.
    • The release of "Arrab al Mowaed" (The Time Is Approaching), a behind-the-scenes documentary of the legend’s record-breaking concert at the MGM Grand Arena in Las Vegas. The show was another record-breaking broadcast in the Arab World.

    • 2000-Present

  • 2000 - Twice at the Beiteddine International Festival.
  • 2003 - Third major multi-state US tour since the 1980s.
  • 2004 - December 12: Tickets sell out completely for 2 venues in Montreal, Canada for February 13 and 14, 2005.
  • 2005 - June 25: Fairuz is awarded an honorary doctorate degree at the American University of Beirut) (AUB) during its annual graduation ceremony. It is the first time a vocalist has been awarded such a high honour, usually granted to avant garde scientists, politicians, and laureates.
  • 2006 - Fairouz donates $750,000 for the aid of the 1.2 million refugees who were left homeless and stranded after the escalation of the war.
  • 2007 - July: Fayrouz broke another record by being the first Lebanese, and artist from the Arab World to perform in Greece, at the Odeon of Herodes Atticus, to a sold out venue filled with 9,000 people
  • 2008
    • January: Fairouz performs "Sah Ennawm" on January 28 through February 5 for eight consecutive nights at the Opera House in Damascus, Syria. The show was supposed to last for 6 days, 2 days were added to the schedule because of the tremendous demand of the Syrians.
    • March: Fairouz performs in Bahrain on 12, March for a 1800 audience, tickets went on sale in the first week of March, 1700 tickets were sold in 30 minutes after release breaking a new record.
